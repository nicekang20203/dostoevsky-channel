Father Murdered

Mikhail Andreevich is killed by his serfs. The patricide that would haunt The Brothers Karamazov.

The Murder

On June 6, 1839, Mikhail Andreevich Dostoevsky was found dead on the road near his estate at Darovoye. The official cause was listed as an apoplectic stroke. But rumors immediately spread that he had been murdered by his serfs.

After his wife's death, Mikhail Andreevich had declined rapidly. He drank heavily, grew increasingly brutal toward his serfs, and may have sexually exploited the village women. If the murder rumors were true, his serfs had reached their limit.

Who doesn't wish for his father's death?

— Ivan Karamazov, The Brothers Karamazov

Forty Years of Silence

Fyodor was seventeen when his father died. For the next forty years, he rarely mentioned him. Only in his final novel, The Brothers Karamazov (1879-1880), did he confront the trauma directly.

In that novel, the dissolute Fyodor Karamazov shares his first name with both the author and his father. He is murdered by Smerdyakov, his illegitimate son — the child of a village "holy fool" whom Fyodor may have raped, just as Mikhail Andreevich may have assaulted the women of Darovoye.

The Freudian Reading

Sigmund Freud saw The Brothers Karamazov as evidence of Dostoevsky's unresolved Oedipal guilt. According to this reading, Fyodor unconsciously wished for his father's death, and spent his life atoning for that wish through his epilepsy, his gambling, and his suffering.
