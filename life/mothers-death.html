27 February 1837

Mother's Death

Maria Dostoevskaya dies of tuberculosis. Two days later, Pushkin is killed in a duel. A double loss that marks the end of childhood.

The Double Loss

In February 1837, fifteen-year-old Fyodor Dostoevsky experienced two devastating losses within days of each other. On February 27, his mother Maria Fyodorovna died of tuberculosis at the age of thirty-seven. On February 29, Alexander Pushkin — Russia's greatest poet, Fyodor's literary idol — was killed in a duel at thirty-seven.

The symmetry is almost too perfect: both deaths at the same age, in the same week. For Fyodor, it was as if the private world and the public world had collapsed simultaneously. The warmth of home and the promise of Russian literature — both extinguished at once.

If I had heard of Pushkin's death as an outsider, I would have gone into mourning. But losing my mother was something else entirely.

— Fyodor Dostoevsky

Engineering Academy

Shortly after his mother's death, Mikhail Andreevich sent Fyodor and his brother Mikhail to St. Petersburg to prepare for the Military Engineering Academy. The grieving boy was thrust from the warmth of family into the cold discipline of military education.

Fyodor hated the Academy. He had no interest in engineering, fortifications, or military life. While his classmates studied technical subjects, he read voraciously — Pushkin, Gogol, Balzac, Hugo, Shakespeare, Schiller. He graduated near the bottom of his class but emerged with a literary education that no academy could provide.

The Void

His mother's death left a void that Dostoevsky would spend his life trying to fill. Throughout his work, maternal figures appear as sources of unconditional love and moral grounding — Sonya in Crime and Punishment, Mrs. Marmeladov's desperate devotion to her children, the mothers who suffer alongside their sons.

Perhaps Anna Snitkina's greatest gift to Dostoevsky was not her stenography or her business sense, but her recreation of that lost maternal warmth — the patient, unconditional support that had died with Maria Fyodorovna in 1837.
